1.4. Ball Work (3 minutes)

  • Dribbling Practice (1 minute)
    • Have players start by dribbling the ball using the inside of their dominant foot.
    • Encourage them to take controlled touches and keep the ball close to their feet.
    • Emphasize using the dominant foot initially to build confidence and control.
  • Dribbling with Non-Dominant Foot (1 minute)
    • After a minute of dribbling with the dominant foot, switch to dribbling with the non-dominant foot.
    • Players should focus on using the inside of their non-dominant foot to maintain control.
    • Encourage them to practice dribbling with both feet to enhance their ball-handling skills.
  • Dribbling Challenges (1 minute)
    • To make it engaging and competitive, introduce dribbling challenges.
    • Set up cones or markers on the training area and have players dribble through them in various patterns.
    • Challenge them to complete dribbling circuits within a time limit or with specific rules (e.g., using only one foot).
    • Add some quick changes of direction and agility elements to simulate game situations.

1.5. Dynamic Flexibility (2 minutes)

  • Leg Swings (Forward and Side-to-Side) (40 seconds)
    • Stand next to a wall or support for balance.
    • Swing one leg forward and backward in a controlled manner.
    • After 20 seconds, switch to swinging the same leg side-to-side.
    • Repeat with the other leg.
  • Hip Circles (20 seconds)
    • Stand with your feet hip-width apart.
    • Place your hands on your hips.
    • Begin by making clockwise circles with your hips for 10 seconds.
    • Then, make counterclockwise circles for the remaining 10 seconds.
